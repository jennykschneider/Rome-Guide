Rome City Guide


The Eternal City

Rome, the capital city of Italy, is a truly unique and fascinating place to visit. Known as the "Eternal City," Rome has a rich history dating back over two and a half thousand years, and it is one of the birthplaces of Western civilization. With its stunning architecture, beautiful public spaces, and thriving cultural scene, Rome offers visitors a wide range of experiences, from exploring ancient Roman ruins to taking in world-class art and music. Home to a diverse and multicultural population, Rome is a bustling and vibrant city that is constantly changing and evolving, making it a destination that is sure to captivate and inspire.

What to see

The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater in the center of Rome built in 80 AD and is known for hosting gladiatorial contests and other public spectacles.

The Colosseum

St. Peter's Basilica is a magnificent Renaissance church located in Vatican City, Rome. It is considered one of the holiest Catholic shrines and is the largest church in the world.

St. Peter's Basilica

Villa Borghese is a beautiful park in the heart of Rome, known for its vast gardens, museums, and galleries. It features stunning views of the city and hosts a variety of cultural events throughout the year.

Villa Borghese

The Trevi Fountain is an iconic Baroque fountain located in the Trevi district of Rome. It is famous for its intricate sculptures and its legend of tossing a coin over your shoulder into the fountain to ensure your return to Rome.

Trevi Fountain

Piazza Navona is a beautiful public square in the heart of Rome, known for its stunning Baroque fountains and architecture. It is a popular gathering place for tourists and locals alike, and features a variety of street performers and artists.

Piazza Navona

The Pantheon is an ancient temple in Rome that is now a church. It is known for its stunning architecture, including the largest unreinforced concrete dome in the world. It also houses the tombs of famous figures such as Raphael and several Italian kings.

The Pantheon

The Sistine Chapel is a famous chapel located in Vatican City known for its intricate frescoes painted by Renaissance artist Michelangelo.

Sistine Chapel

The Roman Forum was once the center of ancient Rome and is now a sprawling archaeological site with ruins of ancient government buildings and temples.

Roman Forum

Castel Sant'Angelo is a fortress located near the Vatican City, initially built as a mausoleum for the Roman Emperor Hadrian and later used as a papal fortress and castle.

Castel Sant Angelo

Where to eat

The signature dish of Felice and Testaccio is Cacio e Pepe.

Felice a Testaccio

Cantina Dei Papi is most known for its cold cuts and cheeses, porchetta, carpaccio of Chianina and Angus, gourmet sandwiches and dishes for vegetarians too.

Cantina Dei Papi

Osteria Da Fortunata serves traditional hand made pasta.

Osteria Da Fortunata

Gelateria Guttilla is a refined Boutique where you will taste the masterpieces of the Sicilan master ice cream makers.

Gelateria Guttilla

If you are looking for huge pasta dishes you cannot miss Da Bucatino.

Da Bucatino

If you want to try authentic Roman pizzas Pizzeria da Baffetto is the place to go to.

Pizzeria da Baffetto

Where to shop

Bordered at both ends by two of Rome's most famous squares, Piazza Venezia and Piazza del Popolo, Via del Corso is the fundamental artery of Capitoline shopping. It is a straight line about a kilometer and a half long that offers items for all budgets and tastes: from the big names or big brands to the cheapest stores.

Via del Corso

Via Cola di Rienzo is a great combination of shops, cafes, gelaterias, and restaurants all in one street. Located in the neighborhood of Prati, this street goes from the Tiber River all the way to Piazza Risorgimento. From luxury shops to markets, you will find what you’re looking for.

Via Cola Di Rienzo

Italy's best-known department store is located in a dazzling space that has seven stories packed with the best luxury goods the world has to offer.

Rinascente

Night Life

Bar del Fico is a charming and cozy café located in the heart of Rome's historic center, near the Piazza Navona. Whether you're looking for a quick espresso or a leisurely aperitivo, Bar del Fico is the perfect place to sit back, relax, and soak up the lively Roman atmosphere.

Bar del Fico

Piazza Trilussa is a vibrant and lively square located in the Trastevere neighborhood of Rome. It's named after the Roman poet and writer, Carlo Alberto Salustri, known by his pen name "Trilussa," and is a popular meeting spot with street performers, musicians, and artists, adding to the already bustling atmosphere.

Piazza Trilussa

Tevere Expo is an exciting cultural event that takes place along the banks of the Tiber River in Rome. The event showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region, with exhibitions, performances, and activities that celebrate the art, music, and cuisine of Rome and its surrounding areas.

Tevero Expo

Profumo Spazio Sensoriale takes sensory exploration to a whole new level, offering a unique club experience that combines the art of perfumery with music and nightlife. The space is transformed into a vibrant club atmosphere, with different scents and fragrances used to create a multi-sensory experience. The music and lighting are carefully curated to match the mood and theme of each fragrance, creating an immersive and unforgettable experience.

Profumo

Art Cafe is a trendy and vibrant club located in the heart of Rome, known for its energetic atmosphere and stylish decor. The club features a spacious dance floor, state-of-the-art sound system, and a variety of bars serving up delicious cocktails and drinks. What sets Art Cafe apart is its unique blend of art and music, with regular exhibitions and installations showcasing the works of local artists and designers. The club also hosts a variety of themed events and parties, ranging from techno nights to hip-hop and R&B, catering to a diverse crowd of music lovers.

Art Cafè

Spazio Novecento is an iconic techno club in Rome that has been a staple of the city's nightlife scene for years. The club boasts a massive dance floor, top-of-the-line sound system, and a roster of world-renowned DJs that keep the party going until the early hours of the morning. The atmosphere is electric, with a dedicated crowd of techno enthusiasts who come to dance and lose themselves in the music.

Spazio Novecento

Italian Hand Gestures Explained

Italian hand gestures are like a language of their own! They are a colorful and expressive way of communicating that Italians have mastered to perfection. From the classic "mamma mia" to the elegant "perfetto," these hand gestures are a fundamental part of Italian culture and identity. So, if you want to really immerse yourself in the Italian lifestyle, start practicing your hand gestures and watch as you blend in like a local.
